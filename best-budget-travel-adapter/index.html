A travel adapter is one of those small items that can make or break a trip abroad. Nothing dampens the excitement of landing in a new country quite like realizing your phone, laptop, or camera won’t charge because the plug doesn’t fit. A good budget-friendly adapter ensures you stay powered up without forcing you to choose between safety, versatility, and portability. When shopping for a budget travel adapter, focus on three things: compatibility (covers the most common plug types, US, EU, UK, AU, and more), number of USB or USB-C ports, and built-in safety features like surge protection and a fuse. Price isn’t everything; even cheap adapters can be reliable if they meet basic safety standards. Below we’ve rounded up three picks that balance cost and performance for different travel styles.
